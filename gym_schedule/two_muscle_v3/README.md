## **Progressive Overload & Periodization Timeline**

| Week Range | Focus | Strategy | Rest Periods |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1-3 | Form Mastery & Baseline | Establish baseline weights, perfect technique, full ROM | 60-90 seconds |
| Weeks 4-6 | Progressive Intensity | Increase weights by 2.5-5% when hitting upper rep ranges | 60-75 seconds |
| Weeks 7-8 | Metabolic Focus | Maintain intensity, reduce rest periods by 10-15 seconds | 45-60 seconds |
| Weeks 9-10 | Peak Performance | Push for PRs while maintaining caloric deficit | 60-90 seconds |

**Deload Week:** Week 6 - Reduce volume by 40%, maintain intensity (same weights, fewer sets)

## **Heat Adaptation Strategy (Delhi Weather)**

| Month | Temperature Range | Training Time Recommendation | Special Considerations |
|---|---|---|---|
| **January 27-31** | 10-22°C | Flexible: 6-8 AM or 6-8 PM | Cooler temps, optimal training conditions |
| **February** | 12-25°C | Morning: 6-8 AM or Evening: 6-7 PM | Gradual warming, maintain hydration |
| **March 1-31** | 18-32°C | Early morning: 5-7 AM preferred | Rising heat, shift to earlier sessions, increase electrolytes |

**Heat Management:**
- Ensure gym has adequate ventilation and cooling
- Supplement electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) as sweating increases
- Monitor for heat exhaustion signs: excessive fatigue, dizziness, nausea
- Consider salt intake increase (if no hypertension)

## **Warning Signs & Corrective Actions**

| Warning Sign | Possible Cause | Corrective Action |
|---|---|---|
| Strength drop >15% on major lifts | Excessive deficit, inadequate recovery | Increase calories by 200-300, add deload week |
| Poor sleep quality (3+ nights) | Overtraining, stress, inadequate nutrition | Review training volume, consider magnesium supplement |
| Excessive fatigue throughout day | Caloric deficit too aggressive | Increase daily calories by 150-250 |
| Mood disturbances, irritability | Low carbohydrates, hormonal stress | Increase carb intake, particularly around training |
| Joint pain or persistent soreness | Inadequate recovery, inflammation | Add rest day, increase omega-3, check form |
| Extreme hunger, strong cravings | Insufficient protein/fiber, poor meal timing | Increase protein, add more vegetables, adjust meal frequency |
| Plateau in weight loss (2+ weeks) | Metabolic adaptation | Add 1-2 extra HIIT sessions or reduce calories by 100-150 |

## **Final Notes**

This is an **aggressive 10-week cutting program** designed specifically for your 15kg weight loss goal during Delhi's weather transition. The plan balances:

- **Muscle preservation** through adequate protein and resistance training
- **Fat loss** through caloric deficit and strategic cardio
- **Strength maintenance** via progressive overload and compound movements

**Critical Reminders:**

**Your success depends on:**
1. Precise nutrition tracking (calories and macros)
2. Consistent training execution (all 6 days)
3. Adequate sleep and recovery
4. Proper hydration (especially as heat increases)
5. Mental resilience during challenging weeks
