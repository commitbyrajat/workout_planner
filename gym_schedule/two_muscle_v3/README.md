## Delhi Cutting Phase Workout Plan — Jan 27 to Mar 31

### Plan Overview

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goal** | Fat loss (target −15 kg), strength retention, muscle preservation |
| **Phase** | Cutting phase with progressive overload |
| **Duration** | 10 weeks (Jan 27 – Mar 31) |
| **Split** | Push/Pull/Legs hybrid — 6 days/week, 1 rest day (Sunday) |
| **Season Context** | Winter → Spring → Early Summer transition (Delhi: 8°C → 35°C) |
| **Training Time** | Recommended: Early morning (6–8 AM) as temperatures rise in March |

## Day 1 — Monday: Chest (Major) + Back (Minor)

**Session Utility:** Compound Strength & Muscle Building | **HIIT:** 20 min post-workout *(chest-dominant; enough time for chest pump to settle before cardio)*

| # | Exercise | Muscle Targets (Count) | Format | Sets × Reps | Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Flat Barbell Bench Press | Middle Chest (1), Triceps (1) | Pyramid Set | 4 × 12/10/8/6 | Primary chest mass builder; progressive overload on sternal head; recruits anterior delts and triceps as synergists |
| 2 | Lat Pulldown (Wide Grip) | Lats (1), Biceps (1) | Straight Set | 4 × 12 | Develops lat width; improves V-taper; antagonist pairing balances pushing muscles and prevents imbalance |
| 3 | Incline Press (Machine) *(Multi-Press — Incline setting)* | Upper Chest (2), Triceps (2), Front Delts (1) | Drop Set | 3 × 10 → drop 20% → failure | Isolates clavicular head of pectoralis major; drop set drives metabolic stress for hypertrophy during cut |
| 4 | Bent-Over Barbell Rows | Lats (2), Upper Traps (1), Rear Delts (1) | Straight Set | 4 × 10 | Compound pull; builds back thickness; erector spinae engagement improves postural strength |
| 5 | Cable Crossover (Mid-Level) | Middle Chest (3), Serratus Anterior (1) | Superset with #6 | 3 × 15 | Isolation finishing move; constant cable tension maximises chest pump; ideal for muscle definition on cut |
| 6 | Cable Scapular Retraction (Seated Rowing) | Lower Traps (3), Rhomboids (1) | Superset with #5 | 3 × 15 | Scapular stability; posture correction; pairs efficiently with chest isolation for time efficiency |

**HIIT Treadmill (Monday):** 20 minutes | Protocol: 1 min sprint (85–90% MHR) + 1.5 min walk × 8 rounds | *Chest pump dissipates within 15–20 min post-weights; back pump not affected by treadmill*

## Heat Adaptation Strategy (Delhi Jan–Mar Transition)

| Month | Delhi Avg Temp | Workout Adjustments | Hydration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **January** | 8–20°C | Standard indoor gym training; full intensity all sessions | 3.5 L/day |
| **February** | 14–26°C | Shift outdoor cardio to before 8 AM if applicable; gym sessions unaffected | 4 L/day |
| **March (Early)** | 20–30°C | Reduce rest intervals slightly to maintain sweat-based caloric burn | 4.5 L/day |
| **March (Late)** | 28–36°C | Train mornings (gym) 6–9 AM preferred; avoid post-noon HIIT outdoors; add electrolytes | 5 L/day + electrolytes |

**Electrolyte Protocol (March):** Coconut water or ORS post-HIIT sessions; add pinch of Himalayan salt to pre-workout drink; banana pre-workout for potassium.

## Warning Signs & Corrective Actions

| Warning Sign | Possible Cause | Corrective Action |
|---|---|---|
| Strength dropping >10% on major lifts for 2+ consecutive weeks | Excessive caloric deficit or insufficient protein | Increase calories by 150–200 kcal; raise protein by 20 g/day; check sleep |
| Persistent muscle soreness (>72 hrs) | Overtraining or poor recovery | Add 1 extra rest day; reduce HIIT frequency to 3×/week; prioritise sleep |
| Dizziness or nausea during HIIT | Dehydration or electrolyte depletion (March) | Reduce HIIT to 10 min; hydrate with electrolytes; avoid mid-afternoon sessions |
| Joint pain (elbows, knees, shoulders) | Volume too high on cut; reduced synovial lubrication | Reduce sets by 1 per exercise; add fish oil; switch to machine alternatives |
| Extreme fatigue or mood changes | Overreaching or caloric deficit too aggressive | Take 3–5 day deload; eat at maintenance for 2–3 days; resume |
| No weight loss for 2+ weeks | Caloric adaptation or measurement error | Reassess TDEE; cut carbs by 20–30 g/day; increase HIIT by 1 session/week |
| Rapid weight loss >1.5 kg/week | Excessive deficit — muscle being catabolised | Increase calories by 200–300 kcal immediately; confirm protein is at 2.4 g/kg+ |

## Key Success Factors

| Priority | Factor | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Protein First** | Never let protein drop below 2 g/kg regardless of calorie cuts |
| 2 | **Consistency Over Intensity** | 6 moderate sessions > 3 brutal sessions that cause injury or dropout |
| 3 | **Hydration in March** | Begin increasing water from 3.5 L to 5 L progressively — dehydration is the #1 performance killer as Delhi heats up |
| 4 | **Sleep 7–8 Hours** | Non-negotiable; cortisol from poor sleep directly causes muscle loss and fat retention |
| 5 | **Progressive Overload** | Log every session weight; aim for +2.5 kg increment every 1–2 weeks on major lifts |
| 6 | **Morning Training in March** | Move all sessions to 6–9 AM as temperatures cross 30°C to protect performance and prevent heat fatigue |
| 7 | **HIIT Discipline** | Do not extend HIIT beyond prescribed minutes — excess cardio on a cut destroys muscle; precision matters |
